Publication
Energy-efficient scheduling for a permutation flow shop with variable transportation time using an improved discrete whale swarm optimization
Xu Xin, Qiangqiang Jiang, Sihang Li, Shuaiyu Gong, Kai Chen
Journal of Cleaner Production, 2021

We addresses a permutation flow shop scheduling problem with sequence-dependent setup time considering a novel conveyor speed control energy-saving strategy. The aim is to find the optimal processing sequence of jobs and conveyor belt speed setting scheme between any two nodes (i.e., machine or warehouse). A mixed-integer linear programming model is established to minimize both the makespan and total energy consumption. To solve such a bi-objective model, an improved discrete whale swarm optimization (IDWSO) is designed that combines differential evolution, augmented search and job-swapped mutation to enhance performance.

June. 2021 - Present (writing)
SR Attack: Efficient Adversarial Attack based on Super-Resolution for Visual Tracking
School of Mechanical Engineering, Tongji University
Vision4Robotics Group
Work with Prof. Changhong Fu

The existing visual object trackers based on DNNs have been shown to be vulnerable to adversarial attack. we explore the adversarial attack for VOT from a new angle, i.e, super-resolution-based attack(SR attack). Our main objective is to online generate adversarial examples by super-resolution(SR) of the search region after direct downsampling, which cause trackers to lose the target in subsequent frames. Experiments on UAV123, UAVDT, DTB70,UAV112 show the effectiveness and transferability of our approach through significantly reducing precision and success rate of the most advanced trackers based on Siamese networks, i.e, SiamRPN++, SiamMask.
